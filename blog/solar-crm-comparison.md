## Aurora Solar: Best for Design-First Workflows

Aurora Solar built its reputation on industry-leading solar design tools. Their shade analysis, 3D roof modeling, and energy production estimates are genuinely best-in-class.

### Strengths

- **Design accuracy** — LIDAR-based shade analysis and irradiance modeling that banks and financiers trust
- **Proposal generation** — Beautiful, design-integrated proposals with energy production estimates
- **Financing integration** — Direct connections to major solar financing platforms
- **Industry standard** — Widely recognized, which helps with credibility for larger commercial projects

### Weaknesses

- **Price** — Aurora's pricing is built for volume installers. Small companies often pay $200+ per user per month, and the cost scales quickly as your team grows.
- **CRM is an afterthought** — Aurora added CRM features, but the core product is a design tool. Pipeline management, lead tracking, and sales workflows feel bolted on rather than native.
- **No project management** — Once a deal is sold, Aurora doesn't help you manage the installation process. You'll need a separate tool for scheduling, permitting, and project tracking.
- **Overkill for residential** — If you're doing standard residential installations with commodity equipment, Aurora's advanced design features may not justify the cost.

### Best fit: Mid-size to large installers (50+ installs/year) who need advanced design capabilities and work with complex roof geometries or commercial projects.

## AccuLynx: Best for Roofing Contractors Adding Solar

AccuLynx started as a roofing contractor CRM and expanded into solar. That heritage shows — it's strong on project management and field operations, weaker on solar-specific workflows.

### Strengths

- **Project management** — Work orders, scheduling, material tracking, and crew management are well-built
- **Roofing + solar combo** — If your company does both roofing and solar, AccuLynx handles both under one roof (pun intended)
- **Document management** — Good at handling contracts, change orders, and compliance documents
- **Established platform** — Been around since 2012, stable product with reliable support

### Weaknesses

- **Not solar-native** — Core workflows are designed for roofing. Solar features feel adapted rather than purpose-built.
- **Pricing opacity** — AccuLynx doesn't publish pricing. Expect custom quotes starting around $100/user/month, with annual commitments required.
- **No solar design** — You'll need a separate tool for system design and energy production estimates.
- **No monitoring** — Post-installation monitoring requires a separate platform.
- **Heavy for small teams** — The interface is built for companies with dedicated office staff. A 3-person install crew running their own sales may find it over-complicated.

### Best fit: Roofing contractors who've added solar services and want one platform for both businesses. Companies with 10+ employees who have dedicated project managers.

## How to Choose the Right Solar CRM

Ask yourself these five questions:

1. **What's your annual install volume?** Under 50 installs — you probably don't need Aurora's design horsepower. Over 200 — you might.
2. **Do you also do roofing?** If yes, AccuLynx's dual-trade capability matters. If you're solar-only, it's wasted complexity.
3. **How many tools are you currently using?** If the answer is "too many," an all-in-one platform eliminates integration headaches.
4. **What's your actual budget?** Be honest about total cost of ownership, not just the base subscription.
5. **How fast do you need to be operational?** Enterprise platforms take weeks to implement. Simpler platforms take days.
