# 5 Solar Proposal Templates That Close Deals Faster

The difference between a 20% close rate and a 40% close rate often comes down to one thing: the proposal.

Not the price. Not the equipment. The proposal — how you present the opportunity, structure the information, and make it easy for the homeowner to say yes.

Most solar installers use either a generic Word document they've been tweaking for years or a bloated 15-page PDF that overwhelms homeowners with technical data they don't understand. Both kill deals.

Here are five proposal templates — each designed for a different sales scenario — that consistently outperform generic proposals in residential solar sales.

## Template 1: The One-Page Visual Proposal

**Best for:** In-home presentations, quick follow-ups, homeowners who want the bottom line

This is the proposal that closes the most deals for small installers. It fits on a single page and answers the only three questions homeowners actually care about:

1. What does it cost?
2. What do I save?
3. How does it work?

### Structure

- **Header:** Homeowner name, address, date, your company logo
- **System overview:** Panel count, system size (kW), estimated annual production (kWh), equipment brand names
- **Financial summary:** Total cost, federal tax credit (30%), net cost, estimated monthly payment (if financed), current electric bill vs. projected electric bill
- **Visual:** Simple before/after comparison showing current annual electricity cost vs. projected cost with solar
- **Timeline:** Three milestones — contract signing, installation, system activation
- **Call to action:** "Ready to start saving? Sign below." with e-signature line

### Why It Works

Homeowners are overwhelmed by complex proposals. A one-page visual gives them everything they need to make a decision without drowning them in technical specifications. You can always provide detailed specs as a supplementary document for those who want them — but lead with simplicity.

## Template 2: The Financing Comparison Proposal

**Best for:** Homeowners comparing financing options, price-sensitive customers

Financing confusion kills solar deals. Homeowners hear about loans, leases, PPAs, and cash purchases — and they freeze. This template lays out every option side by side so they can compare and choose with confidence.

### Structure

- **Header and system overview** (same as Template 1)
- **Option A — Cash Purchase:** Total cost, tax credit, net cost, payback period, 25-year savings
- **Option B — Solar Loan:** Monthly payment, loan term, interest rate, total paid over loan life, 25-year net savings
- **Option C — Lease/PPA (if offered):** Monthly payment, escalator rate, 25-year total cost, savings vs. utility
- **Side-by-side comparison table:** Year 1 cost, Year 5 total, Year 10 total, Year 25 total for each option
- **Recommendation:** Highlighted "best fit" option based on the homeowner's stated priorities
- **Next steps and e-signature**

### Why It Works

When homeowners can see all options on one page with clear numbers, they stop wondering "what if I did the other thing?" and start choosing. The recommendation at the bottom gives them a nudge without being pushy — "Based on your goal of minimizing monthly cost, Option B gives you the best balance of savings and cash flow."

## Template 3: The ROI-Focused Proposal

**Best for:** Analytical buyers, high-income homeowners, investors purchasing rental properties

Some homeowners think about solar as an investment, not a utility bill reduction. This template speaks their language.

### Structure

- **Investment summary:** Total investment, expected ROI percentage, payback period in years
- **Cash flow analysis:** Year-by-year table showing cumulative savings, loan payments (if applicable), and net position
- **Property value impact:** Estimated home value increase based on studies showing solar adds approximately 4% to home value (cite Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory research)
- **Tax benefit breakdown:** Federal ITC (30%), depreciation (for investment properties), state/local incentives
- **Risk factors:** Equipment warranty duration, production guarantee, what happens if you sell the home
- **25-year financial projection:** Total electricity cost without solar vs. with solar, assuming 3% annual utility rate increase
- **Comparison to alternatives:** "If you invested the same $25,000 in a CD at 4.5%, here's what you'd earn vs. solar savings"

### Why It Works

For analytical buyers, a one-page visual isn't enough. They want to see the math. This template provides it in a format they're familiar with — ROI, cash flow, payback period. The comparison to alternative investments is particularly powerful because solar almost always wins on a 25-year horizon.

## Template 4: The Environmental Impact Proposal

**Best for:** Environmentally motivated homeowners, younger buyers, community-focused customers

Not every homeowner's primary motivation is saving money. Some genuinely care about carbon reduction, energy independence, and environmental stewardship. This template leads with impact.

### Structure

- **Environmental impact header:** "Your Solar Impact Over 25 Years" — tons of CO2 offset, equivalent trees planted, equivalent miles not driven
- **Energy independence section:** Percentage of electricity from solar, battery storage options for backup power, grid independence during outages
- **Community impact:** If applicable, reference local solar initiatives or community programs
- **Financial summary:** Still included — environmental buyers want to know it makes financial sense too, but it's not the lead
- **System details and timeline**
- **Personal impact statement:** "By going solar, the [Family Name] household will offset the equivalent of [X] tons of carbon over the next 25 years — the same as planting [X] trees."
- **E-signature**

### Why It Works

When a homeowner tells you they care about the environment, leading with "you'll save $150/month" misses the emotional driver. This template acknowledges their values first, validates the financial case second, and makes signing feel like an act of purpose — not just a purchase.

## Template 5: The Upgrade Proposal (Existing Solar Customers)

**Best for:** Battery storage upsells, system expansions, panel upgrades, monitoring add-ons

Your best prospects are customers who already have solar. They've already said yes once. This template makes the second yes even easier.

### Structure

- **Current system review:** Original installation date, current system size, actual production data (if available), current condition
- **Why upgrade now:** Battery storage for backup power, additional panels for increased consumption (EV, pool heater, home addition), panel replacement for degraded modules, monitoring upgrade for better visibility
- **Upgrade specification:** What's being added/replaced, how it integrates with the existing system
- **Financial impact:** Additional savings, additional tax credits (the 30% ITC applies to battery storage), updated payback period
- **Installation impact:** Timeline, any disruption to existing system, warranty implications
- **Comparison:** Cost of upgrade vs. cost of doing nothing (especially powerful when utility rates are rising)
- **E-signature**

### Why It Works

Upgrade proposals to existing customers have significantly higher close rates because trust is already established. This template leverages that relationship by showing you understand their current system and are offering a logical next step — not starting from scratch.

## How to Use These Templates Effectively

### Match the Template to the Customer

Listen during your initial conversation. The customer's language tells you which template to use:

- "What's it going to cost me?" → Template 1 (One-Page Visual) or Template 2 (Financing Comparison)
- "What's the return?" → Template 3 (ROI-Focused)
- "I want to reduce my carbon footprint" → Template 4 (Environmental Impact)
- "I already have panels but..." → Template 5 (Upgrade Proposal)

### Present Within 24 Hours

Speed matters. The installer who delivers a professional proposal within 24 hours of the site visit wins deals over the one who takes a week. Pre-built templates make same-day proposals possible.

### Always Follow Up

A proposal without follow-up is a wish. Schedule a follow-up call for 48 hours after sending the proposal. Have a second follow-up scheduled for one week later. Most solar deals close on the second or third contact, not the first.

## Key Takeaways

- **One size doesn't fit all** — different buyers respond to different proposal formats
- **Lead with what the customer cares about** — money, investment returns, environment, or upgrading their existing system
- **Keep it simple** — even the most detailed template should be scannable in under 5 minutes
- **Speed wins deals** — deliver proposals within 24 hours using pre-built templates
- **Always include a clear CTA** — make signing the next logical step, not a decision to agonize over
- **Follow up systematically** — the proposal is the beginning of the close, not the end
