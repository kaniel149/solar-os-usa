# Solar Permitting in Florida: AHJ Requirements by County (2026 Guide)

Getting solar permits approved in Florida used to be a nightmare. Some counties took 30+ days. Others required mountains of paperwork that had nothing to do with the actual installation. But thanks to HB 683 — which took effect July 1, 2025 — the permitting landscape has fundamentally changed.

This guide breaks down what solar installers need to know about Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ) requirements across Florida's 67 counties in 2026, including the new five-day approval mandate and county-specific variations that still trip up installers.

## What Changed: HB 683 and the Five-Day Rule

Florida House Bill 683 established the most installer-friendly permitting framework in the state's history. Here's what it means for your business:

### Automatic Five-Day Approval

Every municipality in Florida must now process single-trade residential solar permits within **five business days**. Miss the deadline? The permit is automatically approved. No exceptions.

This is a massive shift. Before HB 683, South Florida counties like Miami-Dade, Broward, and Palm Beach routinely took 15–30 days for permit approval. Now they're held to the same five-day standard as every other county.

### Eliminated Documentation Requirements

Municipalities can no longer demand:

- Copies of your installation contract
- Expense itemizations or cost breakdowns
- Supplementary financial documents
- Any documentation beyond what's needed for code compliance

This eliminates one of the biggest friction points installers faced — jurisdictions requesting irrelevant paperwork to slow down the process.

### Private and Virtual Inspections

HB 683 authorizes two game-changing inspection options:

- **Private inspections** — You can use a qualified private inspector instead of waiting for the municipal building department. Some private providers offer next-day scheduling.
- **Virtual inspections** — Remote video inspections are now explicitly permitted, which can cut days off your installation timeline.

### SolarAPP+ Compatibility

The law explicitly supports automated permitting platforms like SolarAPP+. If your local AHJ uses SolarAPP+, you can often get same-day permit approval for standard residential installations.

## County-by-County Breakdown: What Still Varies

While HB 683 standardized timelines, several elements still differ by county. Here's what to watch for across Florida's major solar markets.

### South Florida (Miami-Dade, Broward, Palm Beach)

South Florida is the largest residential solar market in the state and historically the most difficult for permitting.

**What's standardized:**
- Five-day approval timeline (enforced)
- Private inspection providers available with next-day scheduling
- Combined building/electrical permits in most jurisdictions

**What still varies:**
- **Fee structures** differ significantly between municipalities within each county
- **Structural engineering requirements** — Some Miami-Dade jurisdictions still require wind load calculations specific to the High-Velocity Hurricane Zone (HVHZ)
- **FPL interconnection** requirements are separate from building permits and follow their own timeline

### Central Florida (Orange, Hillsborough, Pinellas, Polk)

Central Florida has seen rapid solar adoption growth, and permitting is generally smoother here.

**Key notes:**
- Most jurisdictions in Orange and Hillsborough counties have adopted streamlined online portals
- Pinellas County requires separate electrical permits for systems over 10 kW
- Polk County has some of the lowest permit fees in the state

### North Florida (Duval, Alachua, Leon)

North Florida's solar market is smaller but growing fast.

**Key notes:**
- Duval County (Jacksonville) uses an online permitting system with relatively fast turnaround
- Leon County (Tallahassee) has a utility-specific process due to Tallahassee's municipal electric utility
- Rural counties in North Florida may have limited inspection availability — plan your scheduling accordingly

### Southwest Florida (Lee, Collier, Sarasota)

Post-Hurricane Ian rebuilding has driven significant solar demand in this region.

**Key notes:**
- Lee County has expedited solar permitting as part of rebuilding efforts
- Collier County requires hurricane-rated mounting documentation
- Sarasota County offers same-day permit review for SolarAPP+-eligible installations

## Required Documentation for Every Florida Solar Permit

Regardless of county, you'll need these core documents for every residential solar permit application:

1. **Site plan** — Showing panel placement, setbacks, and access pathways
2. **Electrical single-line diagram** — Including inverter specifications, disconnect locations, and utility meter details
3. **Structural attachment details** — Roof type, rafter spacing, and mounting system specifications
4. **Equipment specification sheets** — For panels, inverters, and racking systems
5. **Building permit application** — County-specific form (most available online)
6. **Electrical permit application** — Often combined with building permit in South Florida

### HVHZ Requirements (Miami-Dade and Broward)

If you're installing in the High-Velocity Hurricane Zone, you'll also need:

- Wind load calculations per Florida Building Code Section 1609
- Product approvals listed in the Miami-Dade County Product Control database (or Florida Product Approval)
- Signed and sealed engineering documents for non-prescriptive installations
