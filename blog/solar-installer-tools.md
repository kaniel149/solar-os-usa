# The Complete Tech Stack for Modern Solar Installers

Running a solar installation business in 2026 requires more than a truck, a crew, and a phone. The installers who are growing — the ones hitting 100+ installs per year while keeping their margins healthy — are running a tech stack that automates the boring stuff and lets them focus on selling and installing.

But here's the problem: most installers are either under-tooled (still running on spreadsheets and paper) or over-tooled (paying for 8 different subscriptions that don't talk to each other).

This guide breaks down the complete tech stack a modern solar installer needs, organized by function, with recommendations for every budget level.

## Sales and CRM Tools

Your sales pipeline is the heartbeat of your business. Every lead, follow-up, proposal, and contract needs to be tracked — not in your head, not in a spreadsheet, but in a system that alerts you when things need attention.

### What You Need

- **Lead capture** — Web forms, phone tracking, referral tracking
- **Pipeline management** — Visual pipeline with stages from first contact to signed contract
- **Automated follow-ups** — Reminders and email sequences so leads don't fall through the cracks
- **Activity tracking** — Log every call, email, and meeting for each lead

### Options by Budget

**Free/Low budget:** HubSpot Free CRM covers basic pipeline management but requires manual configuration for solar workflows and lacks proposal generation.

**Mid-range:** AccuLynx ($100+/user/mo) offers strong pipeline management especially for roofing-solar combo companies.

**All-in-one:** Solar OS ($49/mo) includes CRM with solar-specific pipeline stages, lead tracking, and automated follow-ups as part of the full platform.

**Enterprise:** Salesforce ($75-$150/user/mo + customization costs) for large operations willing to invest in solar-specific customization.

## Design and Engineering Tools

System design is where technical credibility meets sales effectiveness. Your design tool needs to produce accurate production estimates that lend confidence to your proposals.

### What You Need

- **Roof modeling** — Accurate roof dimensions from satellite imagery or LIDAR
- **Shade analysis** — Tree coverage, neighboring structures, seasonal sun path
- **Production estimates** — Annual and monthly kWh projections based on actual site conditions
- **Equipment database** — Current panels, inverters, and racking systems with accurate specs

### Options by Budget

**Free/Low budget:** PVWatts (NREL) provides solid production estimates for simple residential systems. No shade analysis, but free and reliable.

**Mid-range:** Helioscope ($150/mo) offers good design capabilities with a cleaner interface than Aurora at a lower price point.

**Premium:** Aurora Solar ($200+/user/mo) remains the industry standard for advanced shade analysis and 3D modeling, especially for commercial projects.

**For most residential installers:** Standard residential systems on straightforward roofs don't require Aurora-level design tools. A mid-range tool combined with on-site verification is sufficient.

## Proposal and Contract Tools

The proposal is where design meets sales. You need to generate professional, accurate proposals quickly — and get them signed digitally.

### What You Need

- **Proposal templates** — Pre-built layouts that auto-populate with system and customer data
- **Financing integration** — Show cash, loan, and lease options side by side
- **E-signatures** — Legally binding digital signatures without printing and scanning
- **Proposal tracking** — Know when the customer opens the proposal and which pages they view

### Options by Budget

**Low budget:** PandaDoc ($35/user/mo) or DocuSign ($25/user/mo) handle e-signatures but require manual proposal creation.

**Integrated:** Solar OS includes proposal generation with e-signatures as part of the platform — no separate subscription needed.

**Premium:** Aurora Solar integrates proposals with their design tool for the most seamless design-to-proposal workflow.

## Project Management and Operations

Once the contract is signed, the real work begins. Permit applications, equipment ordering, crew scheduling, inspections — all need tracking.

### What You Need

- **Task management** — Checklists for each installation phase
- **Crew scheduling** — Know who's where and when
- **Permit tracking** — Status across multiple AHJs
- **Material management** — Equipment orders, delivery tracking, inventory
- **Timeline visibility** — See all active projects and their current stage at a glance

### Options by Budget

**Free/Low budget:** Trello or Asana free tiers work for basic task tracking but require manual setup for solar workflows.

**Mid-range:** Monday.com ($24/user/mo) offers flexible project management with good visualization but no solar-specific features.

**Integrated:** Solar OS includes project management with solar-specific stages and permit tracking.

**Field-focused:** Jobber or ServiceTitan for companies that need heavy field service management (scheduling, dispatch, GPS tracking).

## Financial Management

Cash flow is king in solar installation. You need visibility into receivables, payables, commissions, and project profitability.

### What You Need

- **Invoicing** — Professional invoices tied to project milestones
- **Payment tracking** — Deposits, progress payments, final payments
- **Commission management** — Calculate and track sales commissions
- **Financial reporting** — Revenue by month, project profitability, outstanding receivables
- **Tax preparation** — Clean records for your accountant

### Options by Budget

**Standard:** QuickBooks Online ($30-$90/mo) or Xero ($15-$78/mo) for general accounting. Both integrate with most solar CRMs.

**Payroll:** Gusto ($40/mo + $6/person) for payroll and benefits management.

**Expense tracking:** Expensify or Ramp for capturing field expenses and material receipts.

Most solar installers should keep financial tools separate from their CRM — accounting software is specialized enough to warrant a dedicated solution.

## Monitoring and Post-Installation

Post-installation monitoring is increasingly important for customer retention, warranty service, and generating referrals and upgrade opportunities.

### What You Need

- **System performance monitoring** — Real-time and historical production data
- **Alert management** — Notifications when systems underperform
- **Customer portal** — Let homeowners see their system's performance
- **Service ticket management** — Track and resolve warranty and maintenance issues
- **Fleet view** — See all installed systems' performance from one dashboard

### Options by Budget

**Inverter-native:** SolarEdge, Enphase, and SMA all provide free monitoring through their respective platforms. Limitation: if you use multiple inverter brands, you're checking multiple platforms.

**Unified monitoring:** Solar OS aggregates monitoring data across inverter brands into a single dashboard, with alerting and service ticket management.

**Enterprise:** Also Energy or PowerTrack for large portfolios (500+ systems).

## Communication Tools

Responsive communication differentiates good installers from great ones. Homeowners want updates without having to call you.

### What You Need

- **Automated status updates** — Text/email notifications at each project milestone
- **Centralized communication** — All customer emails and texts linked to their project record
- **Review generation** — Automated requests for Google/Yelp reviews after successful installations
- **Referral tracking** — Know which customers are sending you new business

### Options by Budget

**Basic:** Mailchimp (free tier) for email marketing plus Google Voice for a business phone number.

**Integrated:** Most CRMs include basic email and text communication tied to customer records.

**Dedicated:** Podium ($300+/mo) for comprehensive review generation and customer messaging — usually only worth it at 10+ installs/month.

## Key Takeaways

- **Don't over-tool** — The best tech stack is the simplest one that covers your needs
- **Prioritize integration** — Tools that share data eliminate duplicate entry and reduce errors
- **Start with CRM** — If you change one tool first, make it your customer and project management system
- **Keep accounting separate** — Use dedicated accounting software; don't try to force your CRM to be your bookkeeper
- **Monitor what you install** — Post-installation visibility drives referrals, reviews, and upgrade revenue
- **Scale your stack with your business** — A solo installer's needs are different from a 15-person company's needs
