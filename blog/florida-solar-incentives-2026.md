# Florida Solar Incentives & Tax Credits 2026: Complete Guide

Florida is one of the best states in the country for solar — and not just because of the sunshine. Between the federal tax credit, state tax exemptions, net metering (while it lasts), and local rebates, homeowners can significantly reduce the cost of going solar in 2026.

As a solar installer, knowing these incentives cold isn't optional. It's what separates the installer who closes the deal from the one who gets a "let me think about it." This guide covers every incentive available in Florida in 2026, with real numbers your sales team can use.

## Federal Solar Investment Tax Credit (ITC) — 30%

The federal ITC remains the single largest incentive for solar in 2026. It's a dollar-for-dollar tax credit — not a deduction — which means it directly reduces the amount of federal income tax your customer owes.

### How It Works

- **Credit amount:** 30% of total solar installation cost
- **Applies to:** Equipment, labor, permitting, and installation costs
- **Battery storage:** The 30% credit also applies to battery storage systems installed with or after solar
- **Claimed via:** IRS Form 5695, filed with the homeowner's annual tax return

### Real Numbers for Florida

The average residential solar installation in Florida costs between $20,000 and $30,000 before incentives. At 30%, that's:

- **$20,000 system:** $6,000 tax credit
- **$25,000 system:** $7,500 tax credit
- **$30,000 system:** $9,000 tax credit

### Important Details for Your Sales Conversations

- The credit is **non-refundable** — it reduces tax owed but doesn't generate a refund beyond what's owed. If a homeowner owes $5,000 in federal taxes and has a $7,500 credit, they use $5,000 in year one and roll the remaining $2,500 to the following year.
- **No income limit** — Available regardless of income level
- **Must own the system** — Leased systems or PPAs don't qualify the homeowner for the ITC (the system owner claims it)
- The 30% rate is secured through 2032, stepping down to 26% in 2033 and 22% in 2034

## Florida Property Tax Exemption — 100%

Solar panels increase home value — studies show an average increase of about 4%. In most states, that increased value means higher property taxes. Not in Florida.

### How It Works

Florida Statute 196.182 provides a **100% property tax exemption** on the added value from solar energy systems, including battery storage. This means:

- Solar panels add value to the home
- That added value is completely exempt from property taxes
- The exemption is automatic — no special application required in most counties
- Authorized through at least 2037

### Real Numbers

For a $25,000 solar installation that adds approximately $20,000 in assessed value to a home:

- **Without exemption:** At Florida's average property tax rate of ~0.86%, that's roughly $172/year in additional property taxes
- **With exemption:** $0 additional property taxes

Over 25 years, that's approximately **$4,300 in saved property taxes** (accounting for assessment increases).

### Installer Tip

This is a powerful objection-handler. When a homeowner says "won't solar increase my property taxes?" you can confidently tell them no — Florida law specifically exempts solar from property tax increases.

## Florida Sales Tax Exemption

Florida's state sales tax rate is 6%, and solar equipment is completely exempt.

### What It Covers

- Solar panels
- Inverters
- Racking and mounting systems
- Battery storage systems
- Related electrical equipment

### Real Numbers

On a $25,000 installation where roughly $12,000–$15,000 is equipment:

- **Sales tax saved:** $720–$900

This exemption is typically handled by the installer at the point of sale — homeowners don't need to file anything.

## Net Metering — Lock It In Before 2027

Net metering is arguably the most valuable ongoing incentive for Florida solar customers, and it's changing. Installers need to understand the timeline and use it as a sales accelerator.

### Current Net Metering (Through 2026)

Florida utilities — including FPL, Duke Energy, and Tampa Electric — currently offer net metering that credits excess solar production at the **full retail electricity rate**.

This means if a homeowner's solar system produces more electricity than they use during the day, the excess flows to the grid and they receive a credit at the same rate they'd pay to buy that electricity.

### What's Changing in 2027

Under current legislation:

- **2027:** Net metering credits drop to **60% of retail rate**
- **2028 and beyond:** Credits drop to **50% of retail rate**
- **Grandfathering:** Systems installed before 2027 are **grandfathered into current full-retail rates**

### Why This Matters for Sales in 2026

This is the single most urgent sales talking point for 2026. Homeowners who install solar before December 31, 2026 lock in full-retail net metering rates. Those who wait could see their annual savings reduced by 40–50%.

**Example impact:**

A system that produces 12,000 kWh/year with 4,000 kWh exported to the grid:

- **Full retail rate ($0.13/kWh):** $520/year in net metering credits
- **60% of retail ($0.078/kWh):** $312/year in credits
- **Difference over 25 years:** $5,200 in lost savings

That's a compelling reason to act now rather than wait.

## Local Rebates and Programs

Florida doesn't offer a statewide rebate program, but several municipalities provide additional incentives:

### Boynton Beach — Energy Edge Rebate

- **Amount:** Up to $1,500
- **Requirements:** 5+ kW system on primary residence within Boynton Beach utility service area
- **Status:** Check current availability — funds are allocated annually

### Dunedin — Sustainable Development Grant

- **Amount:** $0.25 per watt, up to $2,500
- **Requirements:** Residential solar installation within Dunedin city limits
- **Status:** Available while funds last

### Tallahassee — Energy Efficiency Loan

- **Amount:** Up to $20,000 at 5% interest
- **Type:** Low-interest loan (not a rebate)
- **Requirements:** Tallahassee Utilities customer
- **Note:** Tallahassee operates a municipal electric utility with different rules than investor-owned utilities

### PACE Financing (Statewide)

Property Assessed Clean Energy (PACE) financing is available throughout Florida:

- **How it works:** Solar installation cost is added to the property tax bill and paid over 15–25 years
- **Benefits:** No credit check, transferable when the home is sold
- **Considerations:** Higher interest rates than traditional solar loans (typically 6–9%), and some mortgage lenders have restrictions on PACE-financed properties
