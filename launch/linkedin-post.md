# LinkedIn Post — Kaniel's Profile

---

I don't know how to code.

I've never taken a CS class. Never done a bootcamp. Never written a line of production code from scratch.

But last month, I built and launched a full SaaS platform.

Here's the story:

I own a solar installation company in Israel (Navitas Solar). I co-founded one in Panama (Solaris Energy). I've spent years on rooftops, in utility offices, and in front of homeowners explaining why solar makes sense.

And through all of it, I've been frustrated by one thing: the software.

Small solar installers use 5-6 different tools to run their business. CRM here. Proposals there. Project tracking somewhere else. Documents scattered across email and Drive. Scheduling in yet another app.

Total cost: $400-500/month.
Total integration: zero.

I always thought: someone should build one tool that does everything a solar installer needs.

Six months ago, I moved my family to Thailand. I finally had the time and space to build it myself.

Using Claude Code — an AI coding agent from Anthropic — I built Solar OS in about two weeks.

It's a single platform that handles:
→ Lead tracking & sales pipeline
→ Proposal generation
→ Project management
→ Document storage
→ Crew scheduling
→ Analytics & reporting

Built for solar. Priced for small teams. $49/month replaces $500/month.

The tech stack: React, TypeScript, Tailwind, Supabase, Vercel. All generated through AI. All production-grade.

I registered KANIEL TORD LLC in Florida because that's where the opportunity is. Florida's residential solar market is booming, and thousands of small installers are running everything on spreadsheets and duct-taped software.

Solar OS is live now with a free tier — no credit card, no trial that expires.

The lesson I keep coming back to: you don't need to know how to code anymore. You need to know what to build. Domain expertise is the new moat.

If you're in the solar industry — installer, sales rep, company owner — I'd love your feedback.

And if you're a non-technical founder sitting on an industry problem: the tools exist now. Build the thing.
