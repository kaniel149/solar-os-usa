## Problem Statement

- **Tool fragmentation is killing small installers.** The average solar company juggles 5-8 disconnected tools — CRM, design, proposals, permitting, project management, monitoring, financing. Data gets re-entered. Deals slip through cracks.
- **Enterprise software is priced for enterprises.** Aurora Solar costs $170+/seat/month. Salesforce customization runs $5K-$30K. Small shops with 1-10 people can't justify these costs, so they run on spreadsheets and email chains.
- **Permitting is a nightmare nobody's solved.** Every city and county has different requirements, different forms, different reviewers. A simple residential install takes 3-6 months from contract to PTO because permitting bottlenecks destroy deal velocity.

---

## Solution

- **One platform, zero fragmentation.** Lead capture → proposal → design → permit → install → monitoring, all flowing through a single system. No data re-entry. No tab-switching between tools. Every team member sees the same pipeline.
- **Affordable by design.** Starting at $49/user/month — 70% less than Aurora. No per-proposal fees. No surprise charges. Built for the small-mid installer (1-50 employees) who's been priced out of modern tooling.
- **AI-powered permitting that actually works.** AHJ-aware permit package generation, SolarAPP+ integration, jurisdiction-specific requirements auto-applied. Turn the biggest bottleneck in solar into a one-click process.

---

## Key Features

### 1. Integrated Solar CRM
Full lead-to-PTO pipeline with US lead source integration (EnergySage, Google Ads, Angi, referrals). Track every deal from first contact through Permission to Operate. No more spreadsheets.

### 2. 60-Second AI Proposals
Enter an address and utility bill — get a complete proposal in under a minute. ITC 30% calculation with bonus adders (domestic content, low-income, energy community). Cash, loan, lease, and PPA comparisons. PDF export with e-signature.

### 3. AHJ-Aware Permit Generator ⭐
The killer feature. Database of jurisdiction requirements across the US. Auto-generates permit packages (site plan, single-line diagram, structural calcs). SolarAPP+ integration for instant permits. This alone saves 5-10 hours per project.

### 4. Solar + Battery Design Engine
Hardware-agnostic panel, inverter, and battery selector. NEM 3.0 aware with TOU optimization for California. PVWatts API production estimates. Full US equipment database (Enphase, SolarEdge, Tesla, REC, Q CELLS).

### 5. Unified Monitoring Dashboard
All inverter brands (Enphase, SolarEdge, Huawei, string) in one fleet view. Underperformance alerts, offline detection, error tracking. Customer-facing portal included. Stop logging into 4 different monitoring platforms.

## Reply Templates

### "How is this different from Aurora Solar?"
> Great question! Aurora is an excellent design tool — we respect what they've built. The key differences: (1) Solar OS covers the **full lifecycle** (CRM → proposal → design → permit → install → monitor), while Aurora focuses on design + proposals. (2) We're **70% cheaper** — $49-99/user/mo vs Aurora's $170+. (3) Our **permit generator** is a category of its own — Aurora doesn't touch permitting. We're built for the small-mid installer who needs one affordable platform, not the enterprise customer Aurora targets.

### "What about OpenSolar? It's free."
> OpenSolar is great for basic proposals, and free is hard to beat on price! But we hear consistently that their satellite imagery is poor in many areas, their CRM is limited, and they have zero permitting capability. Solar OS trades "free" for a complete platform — CRM, AI proposals, permit automation, monitoring — at an accessible $49/mo starting price. For most installers, the time saved on permitting alone pays for the subscription in the first project.

### "Is this just for residential?"
> We're launching with residential focus (that's 80%+ of US solar installs), but commercial modules are on our roadmap for Q3/Q4. The CRM and monitoring work for commercial projects today — it's the design and permitting tools that are residential-first currently.

### "What integrations do you support?"
> Current: PVWatts API, Enphase monitoring, SolarEdge monitoring, SolarAPP+ for permits. Coming soon: GoodLeap/Mosaic/Sunlight financing, QuickBooks, Zapier, and major distributor catalogs (CED, BayWa, Soligent). We also have a REST API for custom integrations.

### "How do I migrate from my current tools?"
> We built a CSV importer that handles most CRM migrations in under an hour. For customers coming from Salesforce, JobNimbus, or Sunbase, we offer a white-glove migration on Pro and Business plans. Your monitoring data syncs automatically once you connect your inverter accounts.

### "Is my data secure?"
> Yes — we use Supabase (built on PostgreSQL) with row-level security, all data encrypted at rest and in transit. SOC 2 Type II compliance is in progress. Your customer data never leaves the US (hosted on US-based infrastructure). We're happy to share our security documentation with any enterprise prospect.

### "Can I try it before buying?"
> Absolutely! 14-day free trial, no credit card required. You'll get full access to every feature in your chosen plan. Most installers know within the first project whether Solar OS is right for them.
