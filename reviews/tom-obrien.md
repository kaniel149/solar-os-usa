# Customer Review: Tom O'Brien
## O'Brien Roofing & Solar — Cape Coral, FL

**Reviewer:** Tom O'Brien, Owner  
**Company:** O'Brien Roofing & Solar (8 employees, 15 solar installs so far)  
**Revenue:** ~$2M roofing, ~$300K solar (growing)  
**Background:** 20 years roofing, added solar 6 months ago as an upsell  
**Current Stack:** AccuLynx (roofing CRM on iPad), pen and paper for solar, subcontract electrical  
**Solar Software Experience:** None  
**Date:** March 9, 2026

---

## First Impression of the Landing Page

OK so I'm not a software guy. I'm a roofing guy who added solar because every other customer was asking me about it while I was already on their roof. So I'm coming at this from a different angle than the Aurora crowd.

**What I liked:**
- "Stop paying for 6 tools" — I don't even have ONE solar tool yet, so the idea that I could start with one platform instead of figuring out six different things? That's a big deal. You're saving me from a mistake I haven't made yet.
- The pricing is clear. $49/user is something I can actually budget for. I was looking at Aurora last month and almost choked. I don't need a NASA control panel for 15 installs.
- "Setup in 5 minutes" and "No credit card required" — good. I'm not committing to something I don't understand yet.
- The permit generator feature got my attention. Right now my sub does the permit packages and I have NO idea what's even in them. That makes me nervous. If this tool could help me understand what Lee County wants, that's huge.

**What confused me / lost me:**
- A lot of the language assumes I know solar. "NEM 3.0 awareness," "ITC 30%," "TOU rates," "cash/loan/lease/PPA comparisons" — I know what some of this means from sales conversations, but I had to Google NEM 3.0 when a customer from California asked about it. I'm in Florida, we don't have that. The page needs a "new to solar?" path or at least plain-English descriptions.
- "EnergySage & Angi integrations" — I get leads from Angi for roofing already. If this pulls my solar leads from Angi into the CRM, that's actually amazing. But you don't explain it enough. One sentence isn't going to sell a feature like that.
- No screenshots. I need to SEE what this looks like on an iPad. I'm on rooftops. I'm at kitchen tables. If this doesn't work on a tablet, it's dead to me.
- The testimonials — I've been in construction in Southwest Florida for 20 years. I don't know any of these companies. "Sunshine State Energy, Miami"? Maybe, it's a big state. But it feels like marketing copy, not real people. Put a phone number or a link. Contractors trust other contractors, not quotes on a website.

**What's missing for someone like me:**
- Zero mention of roofing integration. There are a LOT of roofers adding solar right now. We're the fastest-growing segment of new solar installers and the page acts like we don't exist. Where's the "Coming from roofing?" message?
- No mention of AccuLynx, JobNimbus, or any roofing CRM. I live in AccuLynx. My whole roofing workflow is there — leads, contracts, materials, scheduling. If Solar OS can't talk to AccuLynx, I'm running two separate systems and that's a dealbreaker for daily use. At minimum, tell me about your API or Zapier integration.
- No training or onboarding section. I don't know how to size a solar system. I don't know what an electrical single-line diagram is. I need a tool that teaches me as I use it, or at least has video walkthroughs for guys like me.

**Landing page score: 6/10.** Great for experienced solar installers. Not built for the roofing-to-solar crossover market, which is where a LOT of your potential customers are.

## Can a Roofing Guy Who Just Started Solar Understand This?

Partially. The CRM part? Sure, I understand leads and sales pipelines — AccuLynx does the same thing for roofing. The proposal generator sounds simple enough: put in an address, get a proposal. I could probably figure that out.

But the design tool, permit generator, and fleet monitoring? Those assume solar knowledge I don't have yet. I don't know:
- How to pick between Enphase microinverters and SolarEdge string inverters (I've been using whatever my electrical sub recommends)
- What structural calculations I need for solar vs. regular roofing (I know roof loads inside out, but the electrical side is foreign)
- What "rapid shutdown compliance" means in a permit context
- How to read a single-line diagram

The tool needs a "guided mode" for solar newbies. Something like: "You're quoting a 2,000 sq ft home in Cape Coral with FPL service — here's what we recommend and why." Walk me through it. Don't assume I know.

## AHJ and Local Data Check (Cape Coral / Lee County)

I checked if the platform knows my area:

**Cape Coral AHJ:** YES — it's in the database. City of Cape Coral, Lee County, FL. 150 mph wind load, rapid shutdown required, permit fees $125-$350, average 5-day review, NO SolarAPP+ (correct — Cape Coral doesn't do that), and there's even the online submission URL for Cape Coral Community Development. That's accurate. The wind load number is right — we're hurricane country. This is solid.

**Utility coverage:** FPL is in the database with full retail net metering and the correct rate (~$0.152/kWh). That's exactly what my customers are on. HOWEVER — a lot of Cape Coral is actually served by LCEC (Lee County Electric Cooperative), not FPL. LCEC is NOT in the database. That's a gap. Probably 40% of my Cape Coral customers are LCEC. You need to add them. Their rates are slightly different and their interconnection process is different from FPL.

**Equipment:** The equipment database is comprehensive. I see Q CELLS (which my sub likes), Enphase IQ8 micros (what we've been installing), Tesla Powerwall 3, and IronRidge racking (which I already know from roofing — we've used their stuff for decades). Seeing familiar brands makes me feel more confident. The pricing data also helps — right now I'm just trusting whatever my sub quotes me for equipment.

- "New to solar? Start here."
- A 10-minute onboarding video
- A guided first-project wizard

2. Pre-built templates for common system configurations in my area (8kW Enphase system on tile roof in Lee County = here are your panels, inverter, racking, and permit checklist)
3. Tooltip explanations for every technical term. When I hover over "NEM 3.0" it should tell me what it is and whether it applies to Florida (it doesn't)
4. A "Solar Sales Cheat Sheet" I can pull up on my iPad at the kitchen table — when the customer asks "how much will I save?" I need an answer in 30 seconds, not 30 minutes

## Would AccuLynx Integrate with This?

The landing page mentions migration from Salesforce, HubSpot, and Aurora — no mention of AccuLynx, JobNimbus, Roofr, or any roofing software. This is a problem. The roofing-to-solar pipeline works like this:

1. I'm already on the roof for a re-roof job (tracked in AccuLynx)
2. Customer mentions solar interest
3. I want to add a "solar opportunity" to that same customer in my CRM
4. Generate a proposal from the same job record
5. If they sign, track the solar install alongside the roof job

If I have to copy-paste customer info between AccuLynx and Solar OS, it's clunky and my guys won't do it. They'll just keep writing it on paper.

**What would work:** Zapier integration at minimum. API is better. A direct AccuLynx integration would make me a customer overnight. Even a simple "import from AccuLynx CSV" would be something.

## What Training/Tutorials Would I Need?

In order of priority:

1. **"Your First Solar Proposal" walkthrough** (15 min video) — Take me from entering an address to handing a customer a proposal. Don't skip steps. Assume I know nothing.
2. **"Understanding Solar Equipment" guide** — What are microinverters vs string inverters? What panels are good for Florida heat? What battery should I recommend? Written for a contractor, not an engineer.
3. **"Florida Solar Permitting 101"** — What does Cape Coral require? What does Lee County require? What's the difference? What's a single-line diagram and who draws it?
4. **"Talking Solar with Homeowners" scripts** — When they ask "how much will I save?" or "what about hurricanes?" or "is it worth it in Florida?" I need rehearsed answers backed by real data from the tool.
5. **"How to Read a Solar Proposal" breakdown** — I'm sending proposals I can barely explain right now. I need to understand every line item so I don't look like an idiot in front of the customer.
6. **iPad-specific training** — Show me the mobile workflow. I'm not sitting at a desk. I'm on a roof or in a truck.

## What Would Make Me Add This to My iPad Next to AccuLynx?

Here's exactly what gets me over the line:

1. **One-tap proposal from the truck.** Customer just said "yeah, do the solar too" — I pull out my iPad, enter their address, and in 60 seconds I have a proposal showing them their FPL savings. If the AI proposal thing works like described, this alone is worth $49/mo. Right now I fumble through pen-and-paper estimates and email them something 3 days later. By then they've called someone else.

2. **AccuLynx sync.** Even if it's just a Zapier zap that pushes new AccuLynx jobs with a "solar interest" tag into Solar OS. I'm not maintaining two separate customer databases.

3. **"Roofer Mode" onboarding.** A 20-minute guided setup that says: "You're a roofer in Cape Coral. Here are the 3 things you need to know. Here's your first proposal template. Here's what to say to the customer. Go."

4. **Equipment recommendations, not just a catalog.** Don't give me a menu of 30 panels. Tell me: "For a standard Cape Coral residential roof, most installers use Q CELLS 420W with Enphase IQ8+ micros and IronRidge XR100 racking. Here's why." Let me customize later when I know more.

5. **"What Your Sub Needs" checklist.** I subcontract my electrical work. When I start a solar job, I need to hand my electrician a clean package: what equipment, what single-line diagram, what permit status. If Solar OS generates that hand-off package, my sub will love me and my installs will be cleaner.

6. **Offline mode for site surveys.** I'm on rooftops in Cape Coral. Cell service isn't always great. Let me do the roof measurements and photo documentation offline and sync when I'm back in the truck.

7. **LCEC utility data.** Add Lee County Electric Cooperative. A big chunk of Cape Coral and Fort Myers is LCEC, not FPL. Without it, my proposals will be wrong for almost half my customers.

## Bottom Line

I'm the exact kind of customer Solar OS should be targeting: a roofing contractor who's adding solar, doesn't know what software to buy, and will absolutely pay for something that makes me look professional in front of homeowners. I'm not comparing this to Aurora because I've never used Aurora. I'm comparing this to pen and paper. And pen and paper is losing me deals.

The product features sound perfect. The price is right. The data for my area is mostly there (add LCEC!). But the landing page and onboarding aren't speaking to me yet. Talk to the roofers. We're coming into solar whether the solar industry is ready for us or not, and the first platform that makes us feel welcome is going to win a huge market.

**Would I recommend it to other roofers adding solar?** If the guided onboarding existed and it worked on an iPad — absolutely, in a heartbeat. Right now I'd say "looks promising, check back in 3 months."
