# Customer Review: Billy Ray Thompson
**Sunshine State Solar** — Jacksonville, FL
**Date:** March 9, 2026

---

## Who I Am

52 years old. Army veteran. Ran construction crews for 20 years, got into solar 3 years ago. My company is me, my son Tyler, and two installers. We do about 40 residential installs a year in the Jacksonville / Duval County area. Revenue around $800K. I use Google Sheets for tracking jobs, OpenSolar for designs (free version), and pen and paper for permits. I do 90% of my work from my phone.

---

## First Impression

I'll be honest — when the page loaded, it looked slick. Dark background, orange accents, clean. Looks like a real company, not some fly-by-night startup. The headline "Stop paying for 6 tools. Start closing in one." — that hits home because I *am* juggling a bunch of crap right now.

BUT — and this is a big but — I didn't see a single screenshot. Not one picture of the actual software. You're telling me this thing does CRM, proposals, design, permits, and monitoring, and you can't show me what it looks like? That's a red flag. I'm not signing up for something I can't see. I'm a "show me, don't tell me" kind of guy.

The testimonials look nice but they feel... manufactured. "Mike Rodriguez" from San Diego? "David Williams" from "Sunshine State Energy" in Miami? That last one is suspicious because *my* company is Sunshine State Solar. I know most of the solar guys in Florida, and I don't know a "Sunshine State Energy" in Miami. If these are fake reviews, that's a deal-breaker. Be upfront that you're new. I respect that more than fake social proof.

**Readability for a non-tech guy:** Pretty good actually. They speak solar language — AHJ, SolarAPP+, NEM 3.0, ITC. That tells me they know the industry. The pain points section (Aurora costs too much, 6 tools, permitting nightmare) — they described my life. I felt seen.

---

## Is the FREE Tier Enough?

No. 25 leads and 5 proposals a month? I get 5-10 leads a *week* during season. That free tier is basically a demo, which is fine, but call it a demo. Don't call it a "plan."

**What would make me upgrade:** If the Pro tier's AI proposals actually work as advertised — address + utility bill = proposal in 60 seconds — that alone would sell me. I lost 3 deals last month because my proposals took 2-3 days. If this thing really cuts that to minutes, $49/month for one user is a no-brainer. That's one extra deal closed and it pays for itself 100x over.

---

## Is the Language Too Techy / Startup-y?

A few things bugged me:

- "AI proposals" — just call it "instant proposals" or "automatic proposals." When I hear "AI" I think ChatGPT writing my kid's homework. What does AI have to do with a solar quote?
- "Hardware-agnostic designer" — nobody talks like that. Say "works with any brand" or "pick your equipment."
- "NEM 3.0 awareness" — this is fine for California guys but means nothing to me in Florida. Maybe make it clear that's a CA thing.
- "Fleet monitoring" — sounds like I'm running a trucking company. Just say "system monitoring" or "keep tabs on your installs."
- "E-signature built in" — now THAT speaks my language. More of that.

The FAQ section was actually the best part. Plain English, real questions I'd ask. Whoever wrote that gets it.

---

## Features That Would Save Me the Most Time

Ranked by how much they'd change my life:

1. **AHJ Permit Generator** — This is the KILLER. You marked it as one and you're right. I spend 3-5 hours per permit package. Duval County requires site plan, single line diagram, structural calcs, electrical calcs, spec sheets, roof plan, contractor license... I checked and **Jacksonville IS in the database** (id: fl-jacksonville, 130 mph wind load, 7-day review, fees $125-$350). The fact that this thing knows my specific AHJ requirements? That's worth the subscription alone. Going from 3-5 hours to 10 minutes would give me back a full day per week.

2. **AI/Instant Proposals** — 60-second proposals with financing comparisons? If this is real and not marketing fluff, it solves my #1 sales problem. I lost $60K in deals last month from slow proposals.

3. **Mobile Site Survey** — I live on my phone. If I can do roof measurements and photos on-site and have it sync to the project, that eliminates my notebook-to-spreadsheet bottleneck.

4. **Solar CRM** — Getting off Google Sheets would be nice, but only if it's simple. I don't need Salesforce. I need: lead name, address, phone, status, notes. That's it.

---

## Can I Use This on My Phone?

The landing page mentions "Mobile Site Survey" which is good. The page itself looks fine on mobile (I checked the viewport meta tag). But I need THE WHOLE THING to work on my phone. Not just site surveys. I need to:

- Check my pipeline while I'm at a job site
- Pull up a customer's info when they call
- Send a proposal from my truck
- Check permit status

If this is desktop-only with a "mobile app coming soon," that's a problem. I barely open my laptop. This needs to work from Safari on my iPhone or I won't use it.

---

## Equipment Check

I looked at the equipment database. Good news — they have what I use:

- **Panels:** Q CELLS (Made in USA — Dalton, GA — good for ITC domestic content bonus), Canadian Solar, JinkoSolar, Silfab — all there. I mainly use Q CELLS and Silfab.
- **Inverters:** Enphase IQ8+ and IQ8M (my go-to), SolarEdge HD-Wave — check. I'm an Enphase shop so this matters.
- **Batteries:** Tesla Powerwall 3, Enphase IQ Battery 5P and 10T — covered. I do maybe 5-6 battery installs a year, mostly Enphase.
- **Racking:** IronRidge XR100 (what I use), Unirac, SnapNrack — all there. IronRidge XR100 rated for 180 mph wind which matters in Jacksonville.

No complaints on equipment. They've got the brands I actually use.

---

## Deal Breakers

1. **No screenshots or demo video.** I need to SEE the software before I try it. Period.
2. **Fake testimonials.** If those reviews aren't from real people, I'm out. I've been burned by software companies before.
3. **If it doesn't work on mobile.** Non-negotiable.
4. **If the permit generator doesn't actually know Duval County's specific requirements.** The database has Jacksonville, which is promising. But if it generates a generic package and I still have to fix it, that's worse than doing it myself.
5. **If "setup in 5 minutes" is a lie.** If I have to spend a whole weekend configuring this thing, I'm going back to Sheets.
6. **No mention of JEA interconnection process.** Jacksonville uses JEA (Jacksonville Electric Authority) for interconnection. The AHJ database mentions it, but the landing page doesn't talk about utility interconnection at all. That's a big part of the process.

**If the permit generator actually works for Duval County.**

Here's the thing — every solar guy I know in Jacksonville hates permitting. We all complain about it at the supply house. If I could pull out my phone, show my buddy Tommy that I just generated a complete permit package for a Duval County job in 10 minutes instead of 4 hours, he'd sign up that same day. Look, I'm skeptical of software companies. Most of them are built by tech bros who've never been on a roof. But this product — at least on paper — sounds like it was designed by someone who actually understands what small solar companies deal with. The pain points are spot on. The pricing is reasonable. The feature set covers my whole workflow.

If they do those things, I'm in. And I'll bring 5 guys with me.
