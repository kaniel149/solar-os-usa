# Solar OS USA — Review by Priya Patel
**Founder, SunVolt Energy** | St. Petersburg, FL | 2 employees, 25 installs/year
**Date:** March 9, 2026

---

## Background & Context

I'm 8 months into running my own solar company after leaving Tesla Energy. It's just me and my installer, Marcus. I'm doing ~25 installs a year, targeting $500K revenue in year one. My current stack is OpenSolar (free tier), Google Sheets as a CRM, and Canva for proposals. I built my own website, I run my own Google Ads, and I know my way around tech. What I don't have is budget — I can't afford Aurora at $170+/user/month, and my proposals look like a college project next to what SunPower dealers hand out.

I need tools that make SunVolt look like a 20-person operation, not a two-person garage startup.

---

## 1. Does This Make a 2-Person Company Look Professional?

**Yes — emphatically yes.** This is the single biggest draw for me.

The landing page itself looks like it was built by a well-funded SaaS company, which tells me the product probably has the same polish. The proposal system with AI generation, e-signature built in, cash/loan/lease/PPA comparison — that's the kind of thing my competitors at SunPower and Momentum Solar hand over. If I can generate something like that in 60 seconds, my homeowners won't know if I'm a 2-person shop or a 200-person company.

The CRM with solar-specific pipeline stages (Lead → Sale → Design → Permit → Install → PTO) is exactly right. Right now I'm tracking this in Google Sheets with color-coded rows, and it's embarrassing when I accidentally double-email someone because I lost track of where they are in the process.

The permit generator is huge. I spend 3-4 hours per permit package for Pinellas County right now, cross-referencing the building department's PDF checklists. If this actually auto-generates packages with the right documents for my AHJ — that alone justifies the subscription.

**One concern:** The testimonials feel fake. "Mike Rodriguez," "Sarah Johnson," "David Williams" — these read like placeholder names. If the product is in early access, just say "launching soon" or show beta user feedback. Experienced solar people will spot manufactured social proof instantly and it'll undermine the credibility you're building everywhere else on the page.

---

## 2. Is the FREE Tier Actually Useful or Just a Teaser?

**It's useful enough to get hooked, but limited enough to force an upgrade within a month.**

What I get for free:
- 1 user
- Basic CRM (25 leads)
- 5 AI proposals/month
- Basic design tool

At my volume (~2-3 new leads/week), 25 leads in the CRM is roughly 2 months of pipeline. That's actually enough to test the system properly. 5 proposals/month is tight — I probably need 8-10 — but it's enough to see whether the proposals actually look good and close deals.

**Compared to OpenSolar's free tier:** OpenSolar gives me unlimited proposals but the design tool is basic and the proposal templates look dated. If Solar OS proposals are genuinely better-looking with ITC calculations, financing comparisons, and e-signature... I'd trade volume for quality. One beautiful proposal that closes > ten ugly ones that don't.

**What I wish the free tier included:**
- The permit generator, even at 1-2 permits/month. That's the killer feature — let me taste it.
- Access to the AHJ database even if I can't auto-generate packages. Just knowing the requirements for Pinellas County from inside the tool would be valuable.

**Verdict:** Genuine free tier, not a bait-and-switch. I respect that. I'd sign up immediately.

## 4. Would My Proposals Look Better Than SunPower's?

**Potentially, if the AI proposal generator delivers on its promise.**

The landing page claims: address + utility bill → branded PDF with ITC savings, financing comparisons, and e-signature in 60 seconds. If that's real, and the output looks even 80% as polished as SunPower's dealer proposals, it's a game-changer for me.

What I noticed in the codebase that excites me:
- **ITC calculator with ALL bonus adders** — domestic content (+10%), energy community (+10%), low-income (+10-20%). Most tools only do the base 30%. My customers in some St. Pete neighborhoods might qualify for energy community bonuses, and no one is presenting that.
- **Cash/Loan/Lease/PPA comparison** — SunPower dealers always show financing options side-by-side. I can't do that in Canva. If Solar OS generates this automatically, I'm immediately competitive.
- **Payback period and 25-year ROI calculations** — with utility rate escalation and panel degradation factored in. That's sophisticated. Homeowners love seeing "payback in 6.2 years" with real math behind it.

**What would make it definitively better than SunPower:**
- White-label with MY branding (SunVolt logo, colors, not "powered by Solar OS")
- Custom cover page with the homeowner's name and a satellite image of their roof
- A shareable link (not just PDF) so the homeowner can interact with financing options

**Verdict:** Very likely yes, especially if white-labeling is available at the Pro tier.

## 5. White-Label / Branding Options?

**This is where I have a gap in information.**

The landing page mentions white-label only at the Enterprise (custom pricing) tier. That's a problem for me. I'm a brand-conscious founder — the whole reason I left Tesla was to build SunVolt's brand, not to hand my customers a proposal that says "Solar OS" on it.

**What I need at the Pro ($49) tier:**
- My company logo on proposals
- My company colors
- My contact information, not Solar OS's
- Removal of "powered by Solar OS" branding (or at least make it subtle)
- Custom email domain for proposal delivery

**What I'd pay extra for:**
- Custom proposal templates (beyond the default)
- Branded customer portal
- Custom domain for the proposal sharing link (proposals.sunvoltenergy.com)

**This is a dealbreaker if not addressed.** I will not send a homeowner a proposal with another company's logo on it. If Pro includes basic white-labeling (logo + colors), I'm in. If it requires Enterprise, I'm out until I'm bigger.

## 6. AHJ Coverage for My Area

**St. Petersburg / Pinellas County is in the database.** That's critical.

I checked the AHJ data and found:

- **City of St. Petersburg, FL** — ID: `fl-st-pete`
- Wind load: 150 mph
- Required documents: site plan, single-line diagram, structural calcs, electrical calcs, spec sheets, roof plan, permit application, contractor license
- Review time: ~5 days
- Fees: $150-$400
- Duke Energy interconnection required
- SolarAPP+ not available (no Florida AHJ seems to have it)

This is accurate. I've pulled permits from St. Pete building department and these are exactly the documents they want. The 150 mph wind load is correct for our area. The fact that Solar OS knows Duke Energy is our utility for interconnection tells me someone actually researched Florida markets rather than just copying a national database.

**What's missing:** Tampa (Hillsborough) is listed but not Clearwater or Largo, which are other Pinellas cities where I install. Also no Pinellas County unincorporated areas. For a Florida launch, they need more granular Pinellas coverage.

## 7. Lead Source Integration

The lead source data shows EnergySage and Angi integrations, which are the two I care about most. I'm on EnergySage and it's my #1 lead source. If leads from EnergySage automatically flow into the CRM with source attribution and cost-per-lead tracking — that eliminates 2 hours/week of manual data entry.

The lead source analytics (cost per acquisition, conversion rate by channel, ROI per source) are things I've been trying to build in Google Sheets and failing. This would give me actual data to decide whether to increase my Google Ads budget or double down on EnergySage.

Nextdoor integration is a nice touch — I'm active on Nextdoor and it generates 2-3 leads/month, but I have no way to track them.

## 10. Final Thoughts

Solar OS is exactly what the small-to-mid installer market needs. There's a massive gap between "free tools that look free" (OpenSolar, Canva) and "enterprise tools priced for enterprises" (Aurora, Salesforce). Solar OS sits right in that gap.

**For someone like me specifically — a tech-savvy founder bootstrapping a solar company in Florida — this is almost a perfect fit.**

My top 3 requests before I'd commit to paying:
1. **White-label at Pro tier** — even basic logo/color customization. This is non-negotiable for brand-conscious founders.
2. **Product demo or screenshots** — show me what the AI proposal actually looks like. One real proposal screenshot would convert more founders than 10 testimonials.
3. **More Florida AHJ coverage** — Clearwater, Largo, Pinellas County unincorporated. Florida is one of the top 3 solar markets. If you're launching here, go deep.

**Would I switch from my current stack?** If the proposal quality is real, if white-labeling exists at Pro, and if the platform is stable — I'd switch within a month and never look back.
