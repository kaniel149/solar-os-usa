# Customer Review: Jake Martinez
## SunCoast Solar Solutions — Tampa, FL

**Reviewer:** Jake Martinez, Owner  
**Company:** SunCoast Solar Solutions (12 employees, ~150 installs/year, residential only)  
**Revenue:** ~$3.5M/year  
**Current Stack:** Aurora Solar ($510/mo), Jobber CRM ($70/mo), Excel for permits, SolarEdge monitoring  
**Date:** March 9, 2026

---

## First Impression of the Landing Page

Alright, I'll be straight with you. The page looks sharp — dark, clean, professional. Doesn't look like some guy built it in his garage. That matters because I've seen a LOT of solar software startups come and go, and most of them look amateur.

**What grabbed me immediately:**
- "Stop paying for 6 tools" — that's literally my life right now. I felt seen.
- The pain points section nails it. $170/user for Aurora? Check. Tools that don't talk to each other? Check. Permitting nightmare? Triple check.
- The pricing comparison to Aurora is smart positioning. That's the throat you need to go for.

**What made me skeptical:**
- The testimonials feel fake. "Mike Rodriguez" from SunPeak Solar, "Sarah Johnson" from Lone Star Solar Co — I've been in this industry for 10 years and I've never heard of these companies. If these are real, put their actual company URLs. If they're made up, that's a red flag. I'm a business owner — I can smell manufactured social proof.
- "Now available in CA, TX, FL & NY" — available HOW? Is this live software I can log into right now, or is this a waitlist? The CTA says "Get Early Access" which tells me it's not actually available yet. Don't say "now available" and then ask me to join a waitlist. Pick one.
- No screenshots, no demo video, no product tour. You're asking me to trust a landing page. I need to SEE the product. Show me the CRM. Show me a permit being generated. Show me what a proposal looks like.

**Bottom line on the landing page:** It's a solid B+. Messaging is on point but the lack of actual product visuals and the fake-feeling testimonials hold it back.

---

## Would I Switch from Aurora?

**Maybe. Here's my honest breakdown:**

Aurora is expensive and I hate paying $510/mo for 3 seats. But it works. My team knows it. The designs are accurate. The shade analysis is industry-standard. Lenders trust Aurora reports.

To get me to switch, Solar OS needs to prove:
1. **Design accuracy is comparable** — if my designs are off by even 5% on production estimates, I lose credibility with customers and lenders won't accept the proposals.
2. **It actually replaces Aurora, not just competes** — I don't want to run both. That defeats the purpose.
3. **My team can learn it in under a week** — I can't afford a 2-week training curve. We're installing panels, not attending software seminars.

The "all-in-one" pitch is compelling because right now I'm paying:
- Aurora: $510/mo
- Jobber: $70/mo
- Random tools/Excel: unpaid but costing me TIME

If Solar OS at $49/user x 3 users = $147/mo genuinely replaces all of that? That's $430/mo savings. $5,160/year. That's real money.

---

## Features That Excite Me

1. **AHJ Permit Generator (KILLER FEATURE — they're right)** — I checked the database and Tampa/Hillsborough County is IN THERE. They've got the 150 mph wind load requirement, FL Product Approval requirement, the contractor license docs — this is legit. I currently spend 3-4 hours per permit package because every county in the Tampa Bay area has different requirements. Hillsborough, Pinellas, Pasco — all different. If this tool can auto-generate a package in 10 minutes? That's worth the entire subscription alone. I also see St. Petersburg/Pinellas in the database. That's where about 30% of my projects are.

2. **AI Proposals in 60 seconds** — My sales guys currently take 30-45 minutes per proposal in Aurora. If this is even half as fast AND includes cash/loan/lease/PPA comparisons? Game changer. The e-signature built-in means I can drop PandaDoc too.

3. **Solar CRM with solar-specific pipeline** — Jobber is fine but it's built for HVAC and plumbing guys. Having Lead → Sale → Design → Permit → Install → PTO as a native pipeline instead of custom fields I hacked together? Yes please.

4. **Fleet Monitoring** — I currently log into SolarEdge monitoring separately to check on installed systems. Having it in the same platform where I track the project? Smart.

---

## What's MISSING That I Need

1. **Financing integration** — Where's GoodLeap? Mosaic? Sunlight Financial? Dividend? I sell 70% of my projects with financing. If I can't run a credit check and generate a loan agreement from within the platform, it's a non-starter. The landing page mentions "cash/loan/lease/PPA comparisons" but doesn't mention actual lender integrations. Big difference between showing a loan calculation and actually submitting a loan application.

2. **Utility bill OCR / auto-import** — The page says "upload a utility bill" but can it READ it? TECO bills are formatted differently than FPL bills. I need it to auto-extract usage data, not make me type in 12 months of kWh manually.

3. **Interconnection tracking** — Getting PTO (Permission to Operate) from TECO takes 4-6 weeks and is a tracking nightmare. I need status tracking for interconnection applications, not just permit generation.

4. **QuickBooks / accounting integration** — I need invoicing that talks to my books. Jobber does this. If Solar OS doesn't, I'm keeping Jobber.

5. **Actual satellite imagery / shade analysis** — Aurora uses high-res satellite + LIDAR. What's Solar OS using? This is make-or-break for design accuracy. The landing page is vague about this.

6. **Mobile app (not just mobile site survey)** — I need my installers to update project status from the field, upload inspection photos, check in/out of job sites. A mobile site survey feature is a start but I need a full mobile experience.

7. **EnergySage / Angi lead import** — The landing page mentions these integrations exist. Good. But I also get leads from Google LSA, Yelp, and our website form. API/Zapier integration would be important.

8. **Hillsborough County SPECIFIC permit form templates** — Having the requirements in the database is great, but do you have the actual permit APPLICATION FORMS pre-filled? Every county has its own PDF form. The requirements list tells me what I need. I need the actual forms auto-populated.

## Is the Pricing Right?

**$49/user Pro — YES.** This is a no-brainer price point. For 3 users that's $147/mo vs. $580/mo I'm paying now. Even if it only replaces Aurora and Jobber, I'm saving $430/mo.

**$99/user Business — MAYBE.** If I'm growing to 10+ users, $99/user = $990/mo for 10 people. That starts to feel expensive. But unlimited permits and fleet monitoring are Business features I'd want. This is where I'd negotiate.

**The Free tier is smart** — 25 leads and 5 proposals lets me actually test it before committing. That's how you earn trust with skeptical guys like me.

**One concern:** Is $49/user/mo sustainable for YOU? Aurora charges $170 for a reason — satellite imagery, LIDAR data, design engine accuracy. If you're giving me "everything Aurora does plus more" for 70% less, either you're burning VC money or you're cutting corners somewhere. Be transparent about what's different.

---

## Deal Breakers

1. **No financing integrations** — If I can't run GoodLeap or Mosaic from the platform, I need to keep using their separate portals, which means my "one platform" dream is dead.
2. **Inaccurate production estimates** — If a system I design in Solar OS says 12,000 kWh/year and reality is 10,500, I've got angry customers and lender problems. Accuracy is non-negotiable.
3. **No data export** — If I put 150 projects into Solar OS and can't get them out (CSV, API, whatever), I'm locked in. I need to know I can leave if needed.
4. **Downtime during business hours** — We run a fast operation. If the platform is down on a Tuesday at 10am, my sales team is dead in the water. What's the SLA?
5. **No phone support** — I'm not a "submit a ticket and wait 48 hours" guy. If something breaks during a customer presentation, I need to call someone.

## Top 3 Things That Would Make Me Sign Up TODAY

1. **Show me a live demo with a Tampa address** — Let me watch you take a house on my street, generate a proposal with TECO rates, build a permit package for Hillsborough County, and show me the system design. 15 minutes. If it works, I'm in. Don't sell me — SHOW me.

3. **Let me talk to one real customer in Florida** — Not a testimonial on a website. Give me a phone number of an actual installer in Florida who's using this. One real conversation with a peer would close me faster than any landing page ever could.

## Final Thoughts

Look, I'm your ideal customer. I'm paying too much for tools that don't work together, I'm drowning in permit paperwork, and I'm running a lean operation where every dollar and every hour counts. Solar OS is saying all the right things.

But I've been burned before. I tried three CRMs before Jobber. I evaluated SolarNexus, PVComplete, and two other tools before settling on Aurora. Every one of them promised to be "the one platform" and none of them delivered.

What would make Solar OS different? Actually having the features you're advertising, on day one, working correctly. Not "coming soon." Not "in beta." Working. In Tampa. With TECO rates. With Hillsborough County permits.

The data I checked looks solid — you've got my AHJ, my utility, my equipment brands (SolarEdge, Enphase, Q CELLS, IronRidge — that's my exact stack). The foundation is there. Now prove it works.
