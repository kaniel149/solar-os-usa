# Mike Stevens — Solar OS USA Review
**Stevens Electric & Solar · Pensacola, FL (Florida Panhandle)**
**Profile:** 55 y/o master electrician, 6 employees, ~50 installs/year, ~$1.2M revenue
**Tech level:** Flip phone. Son handles the computer stuff.
**Date:** March 9, 2026

---

## First Impressions — Landing Page

Look, I'll be straight with you. My son Tyler pulled this up on his laptop and I looked over his shoulder. The page is dark — like a nightclub. That's fine for the young guys I guess, but it don't exactly scream "we understand a 55-year-old electrician in the panhandle." It looks slick though, I'll give 'em that.

What caught my eye: **"Stop paying for 6 tools. Start closing in one."** Now that's speaking my language. I don't use 6 tools — I use QuickBooks and a legal pad — but I know what they're getting at. The guys beating me on bids show up with fancy iPad proposals and I'm handing over a hand-drawn system on graph paper.

**The good:**
- Price is right there up front ($49/user/mo). No BS. I respect that.
- "No credit card required" — good, because I wasn't about to give one.
- Free plan exists. I'd want to kick the tires before spending a dime.

**The bad:**
- Says "Now available in CA, TX, FL & NY" — but FL means Miami and Orlando, not Pensacola. More on that below.
- No phone number anywhere. Not in the nav, not in the footer. Just "Contact" that links to nothing. That's a dealbreaker for me. If something breaks mid-permit, I need to call a human being.
- Testimonials are from San Diego, Austin, and Miami. Nobody from north Florida, the panhandle, or any rural market. Makes me think this is built for city installers.

## Can a Guy with a Flip Phone Use This?

**Short answer: Not directly. But that's okay — I wouldn't be the one using it.**

Tyler (my son, 28, handles our office) would be the one setting it up and running it day-to-day. The landing page says "Mobile Site Survey" which would need a smartphone — I'd need to get one of those, or have Tyler or one of my guys do the site surveys with theirs.

The real question is whether Tyler can run this thing without being a computer wizard. The page says "Setup in 5 minutes" which I'll believe when I see it. But the concept of one platform instead of chasing paper — I can see the value there.

**What I need to know that the page doesn't tell me:**
- Does it work on a tablet? Tyler could set up a cheap tablet at the shop.
- Is there a phone app or just a website?
- Can I get text/email notifications when a lead comes in, even on a flip phone?

---

## Is Rural Florida Covered or Just the Big Cities?

**This is my biggest concern and the answer right now is: NO, we're not covered.**

I searched through the AHJ database and here's every Florida jurisdiction they have:
- Miami (HVHZ zone)
- Miami-Dade County
- Tampa
- Orlando
- Jacksonville
- St. Petersburg
- Fort Lauderdale
- Cape Coral

**Escambia County? Pensacola? Not there.** Not even close. The nearest entry is Jacksonville, which is 5+ hours east of me. The panhandle might as well be Alabama to these people.

Here's what we deal with in the panhandle that's different from south Florida:
- **Wind loads:** 150 mph (we're in the hurricane zone too, just different than Miami's HVHZ)
- **AHJ:** Escambia County Building Department, City of Pensacola permits — both have their own quirks
- **Gulf Power** (now FPL Northwest Florida) — this is our utility and it's NOT in their utility database at all. They have FPL (which covers the peninsula) but not the panhandle division. Different rates, different interconnection process.
- We also work in Santa Rosa County, Okaloosa County (Destin/Fort Walton Beach), Bay County (Panama City) — none of those are in there either.

If the AHJ permit generator is the "killer feature" (and they're right — it would be), it's useless to me right now. I'd still be hand-building every permit package for Escambia County.

**For the utility data:** They list FPL, Duke Energy Florida, and TECO. My customers are on Gulf Power/FPL Northwest Florida. The rates and interconnection process are different from FPL's south Florida operations. Missing this means their proposals would have wrong utility rates for my area.

---

## QuickBooks Integration?

**Not mentioned anywhere.** The landing page talks about CRM, proposals, permitting, monitoring — but nothing about accounting integration. I've been using QuickBooks for 20 years. Every invoice, every payment, every material cost goes through it.

If I can't export invoices to QuickBooks — or better yet, auto-sync — then I'm running two systems, which defeats the whole "stop paying for 6 tools" pitch. This is table stakes for any small contractor.

They mention "API access" on the Business plan ($99/user/mo) which maybe means someone could build a QuickBooks connection, but I'm not paying double the price and hiring a programmer for that.

---

## Does This Work Offline / Low-Connectivity?

**Not addressed at all.** And this matters a lot out here.

I work rural panhandle. I'm talking Molino, Jay, Century, Milton — places where you're lucky to get one bar of cell signal. If I'm doing a site survey or trying to pull up a customer's project in the field, I need it to work without internet.

The landing page says "Mobile Site Survey — data syncs instantly to the project." That word "syncs" suggests it needs a connection. What happens when there's no signal? Does it save locally and sync later? Or does it just error out?

This is the kind of thing that separates a product built by people who install solar vs. people who build software in San Francisco.

---

## Is There a Phone Number to Call for Support?

**No.** The footer has Privacy, Terms, and "Contact" links that go nowhere (#). No phone number. No email. No chat widget. Nothing.

The pricing page mentions "Priority support" on the Business plan ($99/mo), but doesn't say what that means. Email? Phone? Carrier pigeon?

For $49/month, I need to be able to call somebody when a permit gets rejected or a proposal calculation looks wrong. I'm not going to wait 24 hours for an email reply while my customer's signing with the Alabama guy.

## The ONE Feature That Would Convince This Stubborn Old Electrician

**The AHJ Permit Generator — if it covered Escambia County.**

Here's why: I spend 3-4 hours on every permit package. At 50 installs a year, that's 150-200 hours of my time — or Tyler's time — just on paperwork. That's a full month of work, every year, just pushing paper for the county.

If I could type in an address in Pensacola, pick my equipment (Enphase IQ8+ micros, Q CELLS 400W panels, IronRidge XR100 racking — which are all in their equipment database), and have it spit out a complete permit package that Escambia County would accept? I'd pay $49/month for that feature alone. Twice.

The AI proposals are nice. The CRM would be an upgrade from my legal pad. But what keeps me up at night isn't losing leads — it's the 4 hours I spend on every permit while my competitor across the state line in Mobile does it in 30 minutes with his fancy software.

**Fix the coverage gap. Add the panhandle. Then call me — oh wait, you don't have a phone number.**

---

## Recommendations to Solar OS

1. **Add panhandle AHJs immediately:** Escambia, Santa Rosa, Okaloosa, Bay, Walton counties. This is a real market with real installers who are underserved.
2. **Add Gulf Power / FPL Northwest Florida** to the utility database with correct rates and interconnection info.
3. **Put a phone number on the website.** Even if it goes to voicemail. Old-school contractors won't trust a company they can't call.
4. **Mention QuickBooks integration** — or put it on the roadmap publicly. Every small contractor uses QuickBooks.
5. **Address offline/low-connectivity** — even one sentence saying "works offline, syncs when connected" would help.
6. **Add a testimonial from a rural or small-market installer.** Not everyone is running a 50-person operation in San Diego.
7. **Offer phone-based onboarding** for the free trial. Walk guys like me through it on a call. That's how you win contractors who aren't tech-savvy.
